PPS Generator Startup SOP – Frequently Asked Questions

This FAQ supports the PPS – Service – Generator Startup SOP. It is meant to be used together with the process map and detailed SOP slides.

Questions & Answers

1. What is the purpose of the PPS Generator Startup SOP?

The PPS Generator Startup SOP explains how Palco Power Systems’ Service team handles generator startups from the first customer request through scheduling, performing the startup, and registering the unit.

It connects the day-to-day steps (incidents, SRO, scheduling, pick tickets, shipping feedback, technician work, and registration) to the broader Service-to-Cash workflow.

2. When should I use this SOP?

Use the Generator Startup SOP when:

  • A customer calls or is scheduled for a generator startup.
  • You need to confirm whether Palco sold the generator and locate the original sale.
  • You must create or update a Start-Up Incident and/or SRO.
  • You are scheduling a technician to perform a startup and making sure the unit is registered afterwards.
3. Who is typically involved in generator startup at PPS?

The overall Service-to-Cash workflow involves several roles, including:

  • Service Writers / Coordinators / Supervisors / Managers – handle calls, incidents, SROs, scheduling, and billing coordination.
  • Technicians – perform the startup and note callbacks or additional service needs at the customer site.
  • Parts Personnel – stage the parts that will be needed for the startup.
  • AR / Invoicing / Warranty staff – verify payment, process invoices, and handle warranty claims where applicable.
4. Where is most of the generator startup work actually performed?

Most startups and related service work are performed at the customer’s site. Very rarely is this service performed at Palco’s facility.

5. What triggers the Generator Startup SOP?

The process map shows S1 – Customer Calls for Start-Up as the starting point. The call (or scheduled request) for startup is what triggers the SOP.

During this initial contact, you should gather or confirm key information such as customer details, site information, unit details, and timing expectations.

6. What is the decision at D1 and why is it important?

Decision D1 asks: “Did we sell the generator?”

This matters because:

  • If the generator was sold by Palco, you can usually find the original sale order and related records.
  • If the generator was not sold by Palco, the work may be handled as a different type of billable service, often following a “Service Order Process at Charge” SOP and local billing rules.
7. What is the decision at D2 and how does it affect the next steps?

Decision D2 asks: “Do you know the original order number?”

If the answer is Yes:

  • You proceed to S2 – Order Inquiry using the known order number.

If the answer is No:

  • You proceed to S5 – Order Inquiry Using Serial Number, and if needed, S6 – Create New Unit so the unit is properly set up in the system.
8. Why do we “Check with AR to Verify Payment in Full” (S3)?

Step S3 ensures that payment has been received in full or that acceptable payment terms are in place before committing PPS resources to a startup.

Verifying payment with AR protects both the customer and Palco by avoiding surprises after the service work is scheduled or completed.

9. What happens in “Create Start-Up Incident” (S4)?

In step S4 – Create Start-Up Incident, the Service team creates an incident in the system that captures:

  • Customer and site details,
  • Unit information (order number or serial number-based),
  • Scope of the startup work, and
  • Any notes or special conditions that should be carried into the SRO and schedule.

This incident becomes the backbone for downstream actions such as adding lines, scheduling, and billing.

10. How do “Add Start-Up Notes” (S8) and “Add Line” (S9) fit into the process?

After the incident is created, the SOP has specific steps to:

  • S8 – Add Start-Up Notes – capture technical and customer-facing details that the technician and back-office teams need.
  • S9 – Add Line – add the appropriate charge lines or items to the SRO so that materials, labor, and other billable elements are recorded correctly.

Clear notes and correct lines help avoid rework, billing errors, and confusion at the site.

11. What do “Schedule,” “Print Pick Ticket,” and “Shipping Feedback” cover?

The SOP steps show:

  • S10 – Schedule – assigning the startup to a technician and setting the date/time.
  • S11 – Print Pick Ticket – printing the pick ticket so that parts and materials can be staged and loaded for the job.
  • S12 – Shipping Feedback Ship – updating the system to confirm that parts and materials have been shipped or issued for the startup.

Together, these steps make sure the technician has what they need at the site and that inventory records stay accurate.

12. What happens when the technician performs the startup (S13)?

In S13 – Tech Performs Start-Up, the assigned technician goes to the site, performs the startup according to manufacturer and Palco procedures, and records:

  • Startup results and readings,
  • Any callbacks or additional service that may be needed, and
  • Any issues that could affect warranty, safety, or reliability.

These notes flow back into the SRO and, where needed, follow-on work or warranty processing.

13. Why is “Register a Unit” (S14) important?

Step S14 – Register a Unit ensures the generator is properly registered with the manufacturer after startup.

Registration is critical because it:

  • Ties warranty coverage to the correct unit and in-service date,
  • Supports future warranty claims and service history, and
  • Helps both Palco and the manufacturer support the customer over the life of the equipment.
14. How does this SOP connect to the overall Service-to-Cash process?

The Generator Startup SOP is a specific “sub-SOP” that fits within the broader Service-to-Cash workflow:

  • Customer request / scheduling,
  • Incident and SRO creation,
  • Parts planning and logistics,
  • Technician work at the site,
  • Unit registration, and
  • Invoicing, payment, and (if applicable) warranty claims.

Following the SOP helps keep each of these pieces aligned and reduces surprises for the customer, the technician, and the back office.
