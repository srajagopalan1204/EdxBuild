Sales Order to Pick SOP – Frequently Asked Questions

This FAQ supports the Sales Order to Pick SOP. It is intended to be read together with the SOP slides, process map, and system demonstrations.

Questions & Answers

1. What is the purpose of the “Sales Order to Pick” SOP?

The Sales Order to Pick SOP describes how to work with stock customer sales orders so they are complete, accurate, and ready to move to the picking and invoicing stages.

It covers initiating or maintaining the order, adding items, reviewing taxes and totals, handling special order types such as blanket orders, and preparing the order so that the customer acknowledgement and pick ticket can be printed and handed off to the Pick to Invoice SOP.

2. When should I use this SOP?

Use the Sales Order to Pick SOP when you are:

  • Creating or maintaining a stock customer sales order,
  • Adding or changing line items for that order,
  • Reviewing taxes, totals, addons, and discounts, and
  • Preparing the order so that pick tickets can be printed and the order can move to the Pick to Invoice process.
3. How do I navigate to the Sales Order Entry screen in the training system?

In the documented procedure, you use the following path in Infor CloudSuite Distribution (training environment):

Menu → Sales → Entry → Order

This navigation is used both when you are adding items to a stock customer sales order and when you are creating a blanket order.

4. How do I open an existing stock customer sales order to add or review lines?

From the Order entry screen:

  1. Click Order Number.
  2. Enter the order number (for example, 8567-00).
  3. Select the correct order from the list.
  4. Click Next to move into the order and work with the lines.
5. What are the main steps for adding items to a stock customer sales order?

The documented steps for adding items are:

  1. Navigate to Menu → Sales → Entry → Order.
  2. Use Order Number to open the existing order.
  3. Enter the Product Number.
  4. Select the correct product from the list.
  5. Enter the Quantity.
  6. Click Add to add the line to the order.
  7. Repeat for additional products.
  8. When you are done, click Finish.
6. When should I review “Taxes & Totals,” and what should I look at?

You review Taxes & Totals after you have entered and confirmed the order lines and before you submit and finish the order.

On the Taxes & Totals screen, you should:

  • Click Taxes & Totals to open that view,
  • Use Addons to review or confirm additional charges (such as freight or fees), and
  • Use Discounts to review or confirm discounts.

This step helps confirm that taxes, surcharges, and discounts look correct before the order proceeds.

7. What should I do if taxes, addons, or discounts do not look correct?

If any tax amount, addon, or discount appears incorrect:

  • Stop before submitting or finishing the order.
  • Review the data you entered (customer, ship-to, order type, item lines, pricing, and terms).
  • Follow your location’s local procedures – this may include contacting a supervisor, Credit, or support to correct the issue – before continuing.

This prevents incorrect charges from reaching the customer or causing rework later.

8. When do I create a Blanket Order instead of a standard stock order, and how is it set up?

A Blanket Order is used when you and the customer expect multiple releases or scheduled draws against a single agreement (for example, repeated shipments over time). Follow your local business rules for when to use this option.

The documented steps for creating a blanket order include:

  1. Navigate to Menu → Sales → Entry → Order.
  2. Select the Customer.
  3. In the header, click Order Type and choose Blanket Order.
  4. Click Next.
  5. Uncheck Print Line Price.
  6. Check Lump Sum Bill.
  7. Click Submit.
9. What happens if the sales order is placed on credit hold?

In the overall process flow, a credit hold is a decision point. If the order is placed on credit hold:

  • A notification is sent to Credit for review.
  • If credit is restored or COD / credit-card payment is accepted, the order can move forward to printing the customer acknowledgement and pick ticket.
  • If credit is not restored, Sales is informed to cancel the order and update the reason, under manager guidance.
10. When should I collect credit card or COD payment in this process?

In the process map, collection of credit card or other payment is shown after you have confirmed the order details and Taxes & Totals.

In practice, you should:

  • Review the order lines and Taxes & Totals first,
  • Then collect the COD / credit-card payment or confirm the terms,
  • Then finish the order so it can proceed to pick and invoice.

Always follow your branch’s local procedures for payment handling.

11. How do I print the customer acknowledgement and pick ticket?

After the order is complete and any credit or tendering issues are resolved, the process flow shows “Print Customer Acknowledgement and Pick Ticket”.

In the system, you will typically:

  • Use the appropriate Print or Report function from the order header or actions menu,
  • Select the required outputs (Customer Acknowledgement, Pick Ticket), and
  • Confirm the correct printer / location for your branch.

Follow your branch-specific instructions for where each document should print and who receives it.

12. What is the final step in this SOP and how does it link to the next SOP?

The final step in this SOP is typically summarized as “Proceed to the Pick to Invoice SOP”.

Once the customer acknowledgement and pick ticket have been printed, responsibility moves from Sales Order Entry to the Pick to Invoice SOP, which covers warehouse picking, shipment confirmation, and invoicing activities.
