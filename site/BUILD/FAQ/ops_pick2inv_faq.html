OPS – Pick to Invoice SOP – Frequently Asked Questions

This FAQ supports the OPS – Pick to Invoice SOP. It is meant to be used together with the process map and detailed training materials for Shipping Verification, Shipping Feedback (no change / some change), DQT Proof of Delivery, Pre-Invoice Exception Reports, and Invoice Processing.

Questions & Answers

1. What is the purpose of the Pick to Invoice SOP?

The Pick to Invoice SOP defines how orders move from picked and packed in the warehouse to accurate invoices in the system. It connects physical steps (picking, packing, shipping, proof of delivery) with system steps (shipping feedback, exception checking, invoice processing).

Following this SOP makes sure that what ships from Scott Electric matches what is invoiced, reducing rework and billing disputes.

2. When does the Pick to Invoice SOP start and end?

The SOP starts after the pick ticket has been printed for an order and the warehouse begins working the pick (S1).

It ends when:

  • Shipping feedback is complete,
  • Any exceptions have been reviewed and corrected, and
  • Invoices have been processed and sent to the customer, moving the order into the Invoice to Cash stage.
3. Who is involved in the Pick to Invoice process?

Depending on the location, the process may involve:

  • Warehouse and shipping staff – pick, stage, pack, load, and handle paperwork.
  • Drivers / route personnel – deliver product and capture proof of delivery.
  • Customer pickup counter staff – handle will-call orders.
  • Supervisors / managers – review exceptions and monitor process health.
  • Billing / AR staff – run invoice processing and handle exceptions before invoices go out.
4. What do S1, S2, and S3 represent in this SOP?

The early steps are:

  • S1 – Pick Ticket Printed in Warehouse – signals that the order is ready to be picked.
  • S2 – Orders Picked and Staged (Packed) – product is pulled, checked, and staged/packed for shipment or pickup.
  • S3 – Shipping Verification and Packing – final verification that the right material and quantities are staged and properly packed for the chosen delivery method.

These steps make sure the physical order is correct before it leaves the building.

5. Why are there separate shipping feedback steps and bins (S4 vs S11)?

The SOP differentiates:

  • S4 – Shipping Feedback with No Change – shipments where the shipped quantity and lines match the original order.
  • S11 – Shipping Feedback with Some Change – shipments where quantities or lines changed (backorders, substitutions, cancellations, etc.).

Using separate paperwork bins for these ensures that:

  • “Clean” shipments (no change) can flow smoothly to invoicing, and
  • Shipments with changes can be reviewed more closely for exceptions before invoices are created.
6. What is the role of DQT Proof of Delivery (S10)?

S10 – DQT Proof of Delivery confirms that the goods were delivered or picked up as expected and that proof (signed documents, electronic confirmation, etc.) is available.

Proof of delivery supports:

  • Exception handling (for example, shortages vs. documentation),
  • Customer service when questions arise, and
  • Billing and collections when payment is delayed or disputed.
7. What is a Pre-Invoice Exception Report and why is it important?

The Pre-Invoice Exception Report (S6) lists orders that have issues that could cause incorrect invoices, such as:

  • Missing or incomplete shipping feedback,
  • Quantity or line changes not yet reconciled,
  • Pricing, terms, or tax issues that need review, or
  • Orders with unusual statuses or missing proof of delivery.

By correcting these exceptions before running invoice processing, the team reduces credit memos, rebills, and customer disputes.

8. What happens in “Review and Take Appropriate Action” (S9)?

In S9, the responsible person (often a supervisor or billing specialist):

  • Reviews each exception on the report,
  • Checks shipping paperwork, shipping feedback, and system details,
  • Corrects or completes missing information, and
  • Decides whether the order is ready for invoicing or requires further follow-up.

Only after these actions are complete should the order move forward to invoice processing.

9. What does “Run Invoice Processing” (S7) actually do?

S7 – Run Invoice Processing is the step where the system creates and posts invoices for orders that have passed exception review.

In this step:

  • Eligible shipments are converted into invoices,
  • Revenue and cost postings are made in the system, and
  • Open items are created for customers in the accounts receivable module.
10. What is covered by “Invoice to Cash / Invoice to Customer” (S8)?

S8 – Invoice to Cash / Invoice to Customer covers the handoff from invoice creation to the cash cycle:

  • Invoices are delivered to customers (print, email, EDI, etc.),
  • Any special billing instructions are followed, and
  • The order moves fully into the cash collection process (payments, credits, and collections).

While the detailed cash process may be covered in a separate SOP, Pick to Invoice must be completed correctly for the Invoice to Cash steps to work smoothly.

11. How does Pick to Invoice connect with the previous and next SOPs?

Pick to Invoice is part of a larger chain:

  • It assumes the “Print Pick Ticket” SOP has been completed successfully.
  • It feeds into the “Invoice to Cash” and related AR/collections SOPs.

Thinking end-to-end (Order Entry → Pick → Ship → Invoice → Cash) helps users see why each step, bin, and exception check matters.

12. Why does the SOP mention Infor’s Process Intelligence?

The note about Infor Process Intelligence means that the SOP was not created in isolation; it was built and checked against actual Infor system flows and data.

For users, this means:

  • The steps are grounded in how the system really behaves, not just in theory.
  • Following the SOP helps keep system data, paperwork, and physical operations aligned.
  • Deviating from the SOP is likely to create exceptions and clean-up work later.
